Working Papers
The Causal Interpretation of Panel Vector Autoregressions

Discusses the different contemporaneous causal interpretations of Panel Vector Autoregressions. The interpretation of PVARs depends on the distribution of the causing variable, and can range from average treatment effects to average causal responses. If the researcher is willing to postulate a parallel trend assumption, PVAR can identify ATT. Solutions to SUTVA violations from interference are discussed; applied to natural disasters in the US and Euro Area.

PVAR Causal Inference ATE / ATT Natural Disasters ⬡ PVAR package
Control VAR: A Counterfactual-Based Approach to Inference in Macroeconomics

Under independence assumptions, VARs identify ATE, average causal responses, or a mix of the two. When such assumptions are untenable, Control-VAR uses control variables to estimate causal effects: ATT for binary policies or average causal responses for continuous ones. Applied to natural disasters' impact on the US economy using Germany as a control; results indicate a purely negative economic impact with no cyclical positive effect, contrary to prior literature.

Cointegration Causal Inference ATE / ATT Natural Disasters ⬡ CVAR package
Identification, Estimation, and Inference in Panel VARs via External Instruments

Proposes an IV-based identification scheme for PVARs, introducing μ-LATE: the local average treatment effect arising from a counterfactual generated by a continuous instrument targeting a binary quantity. Anderson-Rubin confidence sets provide reliable coverage for impulse response functions regardless of instrument strength. Applied to fiscal multipliers using military spending instrumented by a shift-share design; estimates approximately 1.7 in the contemporaneous year and 1.5 the year after.

PVAR-IV μ-LATE Fiscal Multipliers ⬡ IV-PVAR repo
Dynamic Local Average Treatment Effects in Macroeconomics with Time-Varying Instrument Relevance

with A. Casini, A. McCloskey & L. Rolla

Introduces a generalised potential outcome framework for dynamic LATEs with possibly time-varying instrument strength. Defines π-LATE: the LATE for the fraction of the population with a strong first stage, providing robust inference across strong, weak, and near-identification regimes. A dynamic programming algorithm identifies the most strongly-identified subsample. Applied to high-frequency monetary policy identification via FOMC announcements, documenting strong statistical evidence of money non-neutrality at the daily frequency.

Weak Instruments LATE Monetary Policy
A Trendy VAR for the Labour Market

with C. Foroni & V. Botelho

Applies Bayesian state-space trend-cycle decomposition to labour market dynamics across the US and Euro Area, building on the BFP framework. Decomposes heterogeneous structural trends (technology, automation, labour supply) from cyclical demand shocks. Developed in part during the ECB traineeship.
